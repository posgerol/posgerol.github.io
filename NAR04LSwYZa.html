Security on a Dating site: 7 tips to help you avoid running into scammers

Even if the Dating site has the best moderation and profiles are checked manually, a fraudster or extortionist will be able to bypass the system and harm users. They skilled, carefully hide their intentions, know how to fall in love with themselves and then deceive.

We have collected 7 tips that will help you not fall for the tricks of a fraudster and understand in time that they are trying to divorce you.

1. Fraudsters are good liars

They write and speak in beautiful, unusual words. They know how to embellish the truth so much so that you will believe every word. For them, the main thing is to quickly transfer communication to messengers, so as not to write on a Dating site with phrases that the system already knows. Standard messages are blocked immediately. "I serve in Afghanistan; I need help for my sick mother; there is a war going on and there is no money for bread; we can't find it in a small town I have no job and nothing to eat; the child needs medicine, I will definitely give the money... " these and other phrases are most often used by scammers. The goal is to put pressure on the pity of a naive user (and believe me, there are enough of them), make them empathize and get more money.

Therefore, if the information seems too strange or if the communication sounds like phrases from movies, if you have requests to send "some money for food" or for a ticket to meet, please inform the site's support service. Similar complaints may have been received before, the support service will check the user and your money will remain intact.

P.S. if you are long enough if you are communicating and planning to meet abroad, never send money for tickets. A proven scheme. Pay for your ticket with a credit card, and you will be able to get a refund.

2. Never disclose passwords, credit card number, personal e-mail, address

Trying to gain your trust, find out your credit card number and passwords (prove that you like me and trust me), find out the details of your online wallet or e-mail address that access data is linked to, and then steal this money from your account is the easiest way to do it.

Fraudsters and extortionists are skilled at manipulating, playing on feelings of a user who hasn't had a relationship for a long time (here, finally met your person) or didn't have experience with Dating sites.

3. Do not send money for the package

An equally common scheme is to say that a new acquaintance bought you a gift, but you need to pay for delivery. The victim sends money (let's say $ 20) delivery service and waiting for a gift. You do understand that there will be no gift? There were cases when the user received an empty box as a gift. The more Dating sites there are, the more new schemes there are. You can't predict everything.

4. Do not send photos of an intimate nature

If you have never met in person, do not send explicit photos. You can't be 100% sure of the intentions of a new acquaintance. Often scammers give 10,000 arguments why they should be trusted, and naive users send Nude photos to show their sincerity and trust. serious intentions, and scammers begin to blackmail him. Make sure that the new acquaintance is an honest and decent person, only after exchanging photos.

5. do Not provide your personal address or place of work

Don't rush to immediately invite them to your home, divulge the address of your place of work, and similar information information. Even if the other person is not a fraudster, but something goes wrong during a personal meeting (for example, he likes you very much, but you don't like him), there is a chance that he will start following you. Wait at home or near work, constantly call, write. You never know what's in another person's head. Know each other in person, and then exchange addresses.

6. Use only verified Dating sites

Use proven and well-known Dating sites and apps. Read reviews and review the agreement. There are Dating apps that in fact, they turn out to be a virus and cause damage to your smartphone and personal data. If your goal is to find a serious relationship, give preference to paid Dating sites and apps, such as RusDate. On paid sites, there are users who also want to build serious relationships, and many times less cheaters'.

7. Make an appointment in a public place

You have been communicating for a long time, nothing in the interlocutor does not strain, does not frighten, it is time to meet. Do not run into trouble and make sure to spend the first few meetings in crowded places, not too late.

It's a good idea if it's a coffee shop. There are also enough people who can "forget" their wallet at home. Therefore, do not choose expensive places and always take money with you. If your gut tells you something is wrong, trust it and stop the meeting early.

Now you know about 7 more safety rules when Dating online. Never give up after the first one failures, keep searching and trust that the right person is there. Online Dating requires patience, time and a firm belief that it is here that your soulmate will be found.
